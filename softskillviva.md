# Soft Skills Viva — Debate Topics

These debate topics are designed to test students on their understanding of soft skills concepts, ethical decision-making, and workplace communication. They align strictly with the 4-unit syllabus from your theory questions. 

## Unit 1: Introduction to Soft Skills

**1. The Promotion Dilemma: Soft Skills vs. Hard Skills**
*   **Student A:** Argues that exceptional technical prowess (hard skills) is the most critical factor for career advancement and product success in the IT industry.
*   **Student B:** Argues that strong communication and leadership (soft skills) are far more essential for stepping into any management, lead, or senior role.

**2. Agile's Demand on Communication**
*   **Student A:** Argues that frequent Agile ceremonies (daily stand-ups, retrospectives) are absolutely essential for team alignment and maintaining project momentum.
*   **Student B:** Argues that mandatory, constant Agile meetings cause communication fatigue and frequently disrupt deep, focused coding time.

## Unit 2: Communication and Interpersonal Skills

**3. The Ultimate Medium: Written vs. Verbal Communication**
*   **Student A:** Argues that rigorous written communication (system documentation, emails, Slack/Jira) is the most vital interaction method in modern, asynchronous IT teams.
*   **Student B:** Argues that direct verbal communication is irreplaceable for resolving conflicts, active listening, and building long-term client trust.

**4. Tech Stack Conflicts: Team Harmony vs. Technical Superiority**
*   **Student A:** Argues that maintaining team harmony through compromise and collaboration should be the priority during architectural disagreements.
*   **Student B:** Argues that fighting for the analytically superior technical choice, even if it creates temporary friction, is the only way to build a robust and scalable product.

## Unit 3: Presentation and Public Speaking Skills

**5. The Core of Innovation: Analytical vs. Creative Thinking**
*   **Student A:** Argues that structured, analytical problem-solving is the primary driver of value and stability in software engineering.
*   **Student B:** Argues that "out-of-the-box" creative thinking and calculated risk-taking are what actually drive tech innovation and market disruption.

**6. Delivering Bad News: Brutal Honesty vs. Empathetic Cushioning**
*   **Student A:** Argues that managers should deliver negative news (e.g., project cancellation, poor performance review) directly and bluntly to avoid any misinterpretation.
*   **Student B:** Argues that leaders must prioritize empathy, using voice modulation and positive body language to soften the blow and maintain long-term team morale.

## Unit 4: Workplace Professionalism & Personal Development

**7. The Ethics of Deadlines: Integrity vs. Business Reality**
*   **Student A:** Argues that occasionally cutting corners (e.g., bypassing minor security protocols) is a realistic necessity to meet critical launch deadlines in a highly competitive market.
*   **Student B:** Argues that a developer must strictly uphold professional ethics and standard security protocols, even if it means missing a major deadline and upsetting stakeholders.

**8. Workplace Culture: Traditional Corporate vs. Startup Casual**
*   **Student A:** Argues that traditional business etiquette, formal dress codes, and structured hierarchies foster a far more productive and professional IT environment.
*   **Student B:** Argues that a relaxed startup culture (casual wear, informal communication, flat hierarchy) massively boosts creativity, authenticity, and overall employee retention.
